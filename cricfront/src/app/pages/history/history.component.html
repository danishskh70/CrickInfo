Historical Timeline

16th Century

Origins of Cricket

Cricket originated in south-east England, possibly as early as the 13th century. The first definite reference is from 1598 when a coroner's court recorded a game being played at the Royal Grammar School, Guildford.

England Origins
1744

First Laws of Cricket

The first known laws of cricket were written in 1744, establishing rules like the length of the pitch and the weight of the ball. The Hambledon Club in Hampshire became cricket's first great club in the 1760s.

Rules Hambledon
1844

First International Match

The first ever international cricket match was played between the USA and Canada at the St George's Cricket Club in New York on 24-26 September 1844.

USA Canada First International
1877

First Test Match

The first officially recognized Test match began on 15 March 1877 between England and Australia at the Melbourne Cricket Ground. Australia won by 45 runs.

Test Cricket England Australia

ICC World Cup History

1975 - First World Cup

The first Cricket World Cup was held in England in June 1975, with West Indies defeating Australia by 17 runs in the final at Lord's.

1983 - India's First Win

India, led by Kapil Dev, defeated the mighty West Indies by 43 runs in the final at Lord's, marking a turning point in cricket history.

Pakistan won their first World Cup under Imran Khan's leadership. This was the first WC with colored kits and day-night matches.
